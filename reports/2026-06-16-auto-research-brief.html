Auto Research: a short field guide

Auto research is not "AI search with a longer answer." The useful version is an agentic loop: plan, search, read, cross-check, synthesize, cite, and revise until the open questions are closed enough to support a decision.

The research loop

The pattern is shared across modern "deep research" products: decompose the question, explore sources, build on earlier searches, then produce a cited report. OpenAI describes browsing, file analysis, graph iteration, and citation as part of deep research; Claude Research emphasizes successive searches and citations; Gemini Deep Research frames the agent as planning, executing, and synthesizing multi-step research tasks.

Where it fits

Market scans, paper triage, competitive landscaping, policy summaries, first-pass due diligence, and "what changed since last quarter?" briefs.

Medical, legal, financial, and security-critical research. Use the agent to collect and organize evidence; keep judgment and liability with a qualified human.

Tasks that require privileged facts the agent cannot access, adversarial source evaluation, or final decisions where one missed citation changes the outcome.

Effort shifts from gathering to judging

The value is not removing the human; it changes where the human spends attention.

A practical prompt pattern

The cleanest prompts ask the agent to plan first, expose uncertainty, and separate evidence from recommendation.

Research: Should we adopt technique X for product Y?

First produce a research plan with:
- subquestions
- source types to check
- stopping criteria

Then execute the plan and return:
- answer-first summary
- evidence table with citations
- contradictions and gaps
- recommendation with confidence level

A simple scoring model for whether to trust the result:

$$Trust = f(source\ quality, coverage, contradiction\ handling, citation\ audit)$$

Risks to design around

Source laundering

A cited report can still overstate weak sources. Require source tiering: primary, reputable secondary, commentary, unknown.

Coverage illusion

A multi-page answer can look complete while missing the key database, paper, regulation, or internal document.

Decision drift

Agents optimize for a polished synthesis unless the prompt asks for "what would change my mind?" and "what is still unknown?"
